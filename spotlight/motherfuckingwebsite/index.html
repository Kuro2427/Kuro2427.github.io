The motherfucking website saga: Peak internet interaction
04/12/2022 1:00 PM

When I first got into web design after watching a video by youtuber Whang on the neocities project, circa 2018, I figured it’d be nice to check the subreddit for HTML, as I do whenever I discover a new interest. The sub was filled with mostly really helpful tips for a beginner web designer such as myself. Heck, it was even there that I found the web host you’re viewing this page through right now! Anyhow, one day, browsing through that same sub, I came upon a legendary webpage, motherfuckingwebsite.com

This website advocated for a cleaner, less bloated web, while also being highly satirical in nature. It argued that a good webpage is one that is comprised of just raw HTML. No CSS, no JavaScript, just vibes, the way God intended.

Though satirical and heavily exaggerated in nature (Though at the time I may or may not have taken this a bit too literally...)


My website at the time...
this site still made a good point. The modern internet is bloated. There’s no dancing around that. And with many people still stuck with subpar broadband speeds all around the world, or god forbid- even dial-up in some places! You can see how general web browsing could become an insufferable slog to some.

However, after the creation of this website, something incredible started happening…

t all started with a site created in direct response to the former, bettermotherfuckingwebsite.com this one argued that with a tiny bit of CSS, you could make your motherfucking website go from an admitted eyesore to something way nicer to look at. However, in classic internet fashion, someone eventually created a response website to the response website! thebestmotherfuckingwebsite.co comes full circle and replaces the minimal design of the previous ones with a fully modern design language; Thus closing the motherfucking website saga.

So, years later, now that the smoke has settled and the meme has died, at the end of the day what did we learn? I’m so not sure myself. I’m just here to show you funny webpages.
